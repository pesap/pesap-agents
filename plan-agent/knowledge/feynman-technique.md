# The Feynman Technique

## Overview
A method for learning and explaining concepts clearly by teaching them simply.

## The Four Steps

### 1. Choose a Concept
Pick the topic you want to understand. Write it at the top of a blank page.

### 2. Teach It to a Child
Explain the concept in your own words as if teaching a 12-year-old. Use simple language, analogies, and examples.

Key constraints:
- No jargon or technical terms without explanation
- No assumed knowledge
- Short sentences
- Concrete examples from everyday life

### 3. Identify Gaps
When you get stuck or use complex language, you've found a gap in your understanding. Go back to the source material and relearn that specific part.

Common gap indicators:
- Using "well, it's complicated"
- Resorting to technical terms
- Saying "you just have to understand that..."
- Hand-waving with vague gestures

### 4. Simplify and Use Analogies
Refine your explanation until it's genuinely simple. Use analogies that connect to everyday experiences:
- Computing concepts → cooking, organizing, building
- Abstract ideas → physical objects, sports, games
- Processes → recipes, journeys, stories

## Why It Works
- Forces true understanding vs. memorization
- Exposes hidden assumptions
- Builds transferable mental models
- Creates durable knowledge

## Signs of Success
- Can explain without notes
- Can answer "why" questions at multiple levels
- Can adapt explanation for different audiences
- The 12-year-old actually understands
